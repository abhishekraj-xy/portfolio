Our Review: 9.5/10

"The greatest cinematic achievement" - Roger Ebert

Fight Club is a bold and thought-provoking film that explores identity, consumerism, and modern masculinity. Directed by David Fincher, the movie follows an unnamed narrator who feels trapped in his boring corporate life. Everything changes when he meets the mysterious and rebellious Tyler Durden. The film starts as a simple story about underground fighting but slowly transforms into a deep psychological journey. Its unexpected twists, dark humor, and intense performances make it unforgettable. Brad Pitt delivers a charismatic performance as Tyler Durden, while Edward Norton perfectly portrays the confused and conflicted narrator. Helena Bonham Carter adds depth with her unique character, Marla Singer.
